⚙️ **COMPREHENSIVE OUTPUT**

**SECTION 1: COMPETITOR PROFILE**
For each competitor in {{competitors}}, create detailed profile:

**Company Overview:**
• Headquarters, founded year, funding/ownership
• Target market and positioning
• Revenue/size estimates
• Recent news (funding, acquisitions, leadership changes)

**Product Capabilities:**
• Core features and functionality
• Strengths (where they excel)
• Weaknesses (limitations, gaps vs. {{product_description}})
• Roadmap insights (public commitments)

**Go-to-Market:**
• Pricing model and typical deal sizes
• Sales approach (self-serve, inside sales, enterprise)
• Target personas and ideal customer profile
• Key messaging and value props

**SECTION 2: HEAD-TO-HEAD COMPARISON**
Side-by-side feature comparison:

| Capability | {{company_name}} | [Competitor] | Winner |
|------------|------------------|--------------|---------|
| [Feature 1 from {{value_propositions}}] | ✅ Best-in-class | ⚠️ Limited | Us |
| [Feature 2] | ✅ Native | ❌ Via integration | Us |
| [Feature 3] | ✅ Included | 💰 Extra cost | Us |

Highlight areas where {{unique_differentiators}} shine.

**SECTION 3: WIN THEMES**
Core messages to emphasize against each competitor:

**vs. [Competitor A]:**
• **Win Theme 1:** "Purpose-built for {{industry}}" (they're horizontal, we're vertical)
• **Win Theme 2:** "10x faster implementation" (they take months, we take weeks)
• **Win Theme 3:** "All-in-one vs. point solution" (they require 3 tools, we're 1)

Tie themes to {{value_propositions}} and {{pain_points}}.

**SECTION 4: OBJECTION HANDLING**
Address common competitive objections:

**Objection:** "We're already using [Competitor]"
**Response:** "That's great—many of our best customers came from [Competitor]. What they found was [specific limitation]. Let me show you how we solve that with [{{unique_differentiators}}]. Can I ask, on a scale of 1-10, how happy are you with [their weak point]?"

**Objection:** "[Competitor] is cheaper"
**Response:** "Let me show you the total cost. [Competitor] charges extra for [features], requires [additional tools], and takes [X longer] to implement. When customers do the math, we're actually 30% less expensive and deliver value [Y months faster]. Here's an ROI calculator based on your situation..."

**Objection:** "[Competitor] is the market leader"
**Response:** "They pioneered the category, which we respect. But the market's evolved. Today's {{target_personas}} need [modern capability]. That's why {{company_name}} was built—specifically to address {{pain_points}} that legacy players can't. Let me show you why [customer name] recently switched..."

**SECTION 5: LANDMINE QUESTIONS**
Questions to ask prospects that expose competitor weaknesses:

**vs. [Legacy/Established Competitor]:**
• "How long does it take to [key workflow]?" (exposes slow UX)
• "Can you [modern feature] without custom code?" (exposes tech debt)
• "How's your mobile experience?" (exposes weak mobile)

**vs. [Startup Competitor]:**
• "What's your uptime SLA?" (exposes reliability concerns)
• "How many customers like us ({{average_deal_size}} size) do you have?" (exposes lack of scale)
• "What happens if you get acquired or shut down?" (exposes risk)

**SECTION 6: PROOF POINTS**
Customer evidence of competitive wins:
• Case study: "[Customer name] switched from [Competitor] and achieved [X% improvement]"
• Win quote: "[Quote from executive about why they chose us]"
• ROI data: "Customers switching from [Competitor] see average payback in [X months]"
• Review scores: "We have [X stars] on G2 vs their [Y stars]"
