## STEP 1: STRATEGIC LANDING (Months 0-3)

The "land" phase sets the foundation for future expansion. How you enter an account determines your expansion potential.

### **LANDING STRATEGY DESIGN**

**Deal Structuring for Expansion:**

The initial deal should be:
- **Quick to close:** Minimize friction, focus on specific {{pain_points}}
- **Fast to value:** Deliver measurable results within 30-60 days
- **Expandable by design:** Leave obvious room for growth
- **Championship-building:** Create internal advocates for future expansion

**Ideal Landing Motion:**

**Small Department/Team Entry:**
- Start with 5-25 users in single department
- Focus on acute pain point from {{pain_points}}
- Price to minimize approval hurdles (under departmental budget threshold)
- Timeline: Close within {{sales_cycle_length}} or faster

**Example Landing Scenarios:**

**Scenario A - Department Pilot:**
- Land with: Sales team (20 seats)
- Problem solved: {{pain_points}}
- Initial ARR: {{average_deal_size}}
- Expansion path: Full sales org → Marketing → Customer Success → Enterprise-wide

**Scenario B - Use Case Entry:**
- Land with: Specific workflow/process
- Problem solved: Specific {{pain_points}}
- Initial ARR: {{average_deal_size}}
- Expansion path: Adjacent workflows → Other departments → Full platform

**Landing Deal Structure:**

**Pricing Strategy:**
- Offer attractive entry price (discounted to land)
- Short initial term (annual preferred over multi-year for first contract)
- Build in expansion triggers (volume discounts, enterprise features visible)
- Make expansion pricing transparent and compelling

**Contract Terms:**
- Include language allowing mid-term expansions
- Set clear success metrics for initial deployment
- Executive sponsor requirement (senior stakeholder buy-in)
- Quarterly business review (QBR) commitment

**Success Criteria Definition:**

Establish measurable goals for initial 90 days:
- Adoption: _____% of purchased seats active weekly
- Value realization: Achieve specific outcome from {{value_propositions}}
- Satisfaction: NPS score of _____ or higher
- Engagement: Regular usage of _____ core features

**Champion Identification & Development:**

During sales process, identify:
- **Executive Sponsor:** C-level or VP who can authorize expansion (from {{target_personas}})
- **Champion:** Day-to-day advocate who loves the product
- **Technical Buyer:** IT/ops person who evaluates and implements
- **Economic Buyer:** Budget holder for department/company

Cultivate champion by:
- Making them successful and visible internally
- Providing data/materials they can use to advocate
- Including them in product roadmap discussions
- Recognizing their influence and importance

## STEP 2: VALUE REALIZATION & ADOPTION (Months 1-6)

The critical phase: deliver exceptional value to build foundation for expansion.

### **ONBOARDING FOR EXPANSION**

**90-Day Onboarding Sprint:**

**Week 1-2: Rapid Implementation**
- Deploy product to initial users
- Admin/champion training completed
- Core integrations configured (with {{tech_stack}} if applicable)
- Success metrics baseline established

**Week 3-4: Initial Adoption**
- End-user training sessions
- Daily usage tracking and intervention
- Quick-win identification and celebration
- Support tickets resolved within 24 hours

**Week 5-8: Habit Formation**
- Power user development program
- Best practices sharing (from similar {{industry}} customers)
- Feature adoption campaigns
- Weekly check-ins with champion

**Week 9-12: Value Proof**
- Generate first ROI report showing {{value_propositions}} delivered
- Executive business review with sponsor
- Case study/testimonial capture
- Expansion planning conversation initiated

**Metrics Tracking:**

Monitor these indicators of expansion readiness:
- **Adoption metrics:** DAU/MAU, feature breadth, login frequency, power users
- **Value metrics:** Business outcomes from {{value_propositions}}, ROI validated
- **Health metrics:** NPS scores, support tickets, feedback quality
- **Expansion signals:** Seat requests, feature inquiries, new stakeholders

**Adoption Acceleration Tactics:**

If adoption is lagging:
- **Power user program:** Identify top 3-5 users, make them champions
- **Targeted training:** Address specific skill/knowledge gaps
- **Workflow integration:** Embed {{product_description}} into daily work
- **Gamification:** Leaderboards, challenges, recognition for usage
- **Executive engagement:** Have sponsor reinforce importance/priority

**Value Demonstration:**

Create compelling proof points:
- **Quantified ROI:** "$X saved, Y hours reclaimed, Z% improvement"
- **Before/after comparison:** Show transformation with data
- **Peer benchmarks:** "Top quartile performance vs. similar {{industry}} companies"
- **Visual dashboards:** Make value visible and shareable
- **Success stories:** Document wins from individual users

## STEP 3: EXPANSION READINESS ASSESSMENT (Month 6)

Systematically evaluate if account is ready for expansion outreach.

### **EXPANSION READINESS SCORECARD**

**Score each dimension 1-5 (5 = excellent, 1 = poor):**

**1. Product Adoption** (__/5)
- 5: 90%+ seats active weekly, deep feature usage
- 4: 70-89% active, good feature adoption
- 3: 50-69% active, basic features used
- 2: 30-49% active, limited engagement
- 1: <30% active, poor adoption

**2. Value Realization** (__/5)
- 5: Exceeded ROI targets, measurable business impact
- 4: Met ROI targets, clear value delivered
- 3: Moderate value, some wins
- 2: Limited value, struggling to show ROI
- 1: No clear value delivered yet

**3. Customer Health** (__/5)
- 5: NPS 9-10, active advocates, zero churn risk
- 4: NPS 7-8, satisfied, low churn risk
- 3: NPS 5-6, neutral, moderate risk
- 2: NPS 3-4, concerns, elevated risk
- 1: NPS 0-2, at-risk, high churn probability

**4. Relationship Strength** (__/5)
- 5: C-suite engaged, champion actively advocating, trusted advisor status
- 4: Executive sponsor supportive, good champion relationship
- 3: Champion relationship solid, limited executive engagement
- 2: Tactical relationship only, champion weak
- 1: Transactional only, no real partnership

**5. Expansion Opportunity** (__/5)
- 5: 10X+ potential, multiple products/depts, strategic fit
- 4: 5-10X potential, clear expansion paths
- 3: 2-5X potential, some opportunities
- 2: <2X potential, limited whitespace
- 1: Minimal expansion opportunity

**6. Organizational Readiness** (__/5)
- 5: Budget available, growing company, urgent needs
- 4: Budget likely, stable growth, good timing
- 3: Budget uncertain, moderate need
- 2: Budget constrained, low urgency
- 1: Budget frozen, no near-term opportunity

**TOTAL SCORE: ____/30**

**Readiness Assessment:**
- **25-30 points:** READY - Pursue expansion immediately
- **20-24 points:** NEARLY READY - Address gaps, then expand within 90 days
- **15-19 points:** NOT YET - Focus on adoption & value for 90+ days
- **<15 points:** AT RISK - Stabilize account before considering expansion

## STEP 4: MULTI-PHASE EXPANSION EXECUTION (Months 6-24)

Systematic approach to growing account value through staged expansions.

### **EXPANSION WAVE 1: DEPARTMENT/SEAT GROWTH (Months 6-9)**

**Expand within initial department:**

**Trigger Signals:**
- Initial users are power users (high engagement)
- New hires joining team need seats
- Champion requesting more seats
- Department reorganization/growth

**Expansion Play:**
- **What:** Add seats to existing department
- **Value prop:** Maintain team collaboration, scale proven ROI
- **Pricing:** Volume discount (e.g., 20% off additional seats)
- **Stakeholder:** Department head (already a champion)
- **Close strategy:** Simple amendment to existing contract
- **Timeline:** 2-4 weeks to close

### **EXPANSION WAVE 2: ADJACENT DEPARTMENT (Months 9-12)**

**Cross-sell to related department:**

**Trigger Signals:**
- Success stories reaching other departments
- Adjacent team has similar {{pain_points}}
- Champion introducing {{company_name}} to peers
- Company initiative requiring cross-functional tools

**Expansion Play:**
- **What:** New department (e.g., Marketing after landing Sales)
- **Value prop:** Solve similar problems, leverage existing investment
- **Pricing:** New department pricing, possible multi-dept discount
- **Stakeholder:** New department head + existing executive sponsor
- **Close strategy:** Pilot or proof of concept in new dept
- **Timeline:** 4-8 weeks

### **EXPANSION WAVE 3: PRODUCT/TIER EXPANSION (Months 12-18)**

**Upsell to higher tier or cross-sell additional products:**

**Trigger Signals:**
- Hitting usage limits (seats, storage, API calls)
- Requesting advanced features only in higher tiers
- Adjacent use cases requiring different product
- Competitor evaluation for capabilities {{company_name}} offers

**Expansion Play:**
- **What:** Upgrade to Enterprise tier OR add Product B from portfolio
- **Value prop:** Unlock advanced capabilities, consolidated platform
- **Pricing:** Tier upgrade or bundle discount
- **Stakeholder:** Executive sponsor + IT/procurement
- **Close strategy:** Business case showing ROI of advanced features
- **Timeline:** 8-12 weeks

### **EXPANSION WAVE 4: ENTERPRISE-WIDE DEPLOYMENT (Months 18-24)**

**Company-wide standardization:**

**Trigger Signals:**
- Success across 3+ departments
- Executive mandate for company-wide platform
- M&A creating need for consolidated systems
- Competitor displacement opportunity
- Budget cycle creating strategic investment window

**Expansion Play:**
- **What:** Enterprise license, all departments, global deployment
- **Value prop:** Company-wide efficiency, data consolidation, strategic partnership
- **Pricing:** Enterprise agreement, significant volume discount
- **Stakeholder:** C-suite (CFO, CIO, COO) + procurement
- **Close strategy:** Multi-year strategic partnership
- **Timeline:** 3-6 months

### **CONTINUOUS EXPANSION TACTICS**

**Even fully-penetrated accounts can grow:**

**1. Usage-Based Expansion:**
- Increase consumption (API calls, storage, transactions)
- Seasonal spikes requiring temporary capacity
- Growth in user count as company hires

**2. New Product Innovation:**
- Launch new products addressing adjacent needs
- Cross-sell emerging offerings to installed base
- Bundle new capabilities

**3. Geographic Expansion:**
- Expand to new regions as customer grows globally
- Deploy in acquired companies post-M&A

**4. Multi-Year Extensions:**
- Renew into longer-term commitments (3-5 years)
- Lock in pricing for budget certainty

**5. Services & Support Expansion:**
- Premium support tiers, professional services
- Training and certification programs
- Consulting and strategic advisory
