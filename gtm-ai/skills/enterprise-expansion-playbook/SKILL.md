## STEP 3: POLITICAL LANDSCAPE NAVIGATION

Enterprise expansion is as much about organizational politics as product value.

### **POLITICAL DYNAMICS ASSESSMENT**

**Power Structures:**

**Formal Power (Org Chart):**
- Decision authority follows reporting lines
- Budget control by level (BU vs. corporate)
- Approval processes and thresholds

**Informal Power (Political Reality):**
- Who does the CEO trust most? (Inner circle)
- Which BUs are favored/strategic vs. disfavored?
- Rising stars vs. entrenched leaders
- Personal relationships crossing BU lines
- Historical rivalries or alliances

**Coalition Building:**

**Identify Natural Allies:**
- BUs with similar challenges from {{pain_points}}
- Leaders who collaborate regularly
- Functions with shared objectives from {{key_goals}}
- Geographies with common needs

**Build Expansion Coalition:**

**Stage 1: Initial Champions (Month 1-3)**
- Current BU success story fully documented
- Current executive sponsor actively advocating
- 2-3 power users willing to be references
- ROI data quantified and shareable

**Stage 2: Early Adopter BUs (Month 4-6)**
- Identify 1-2 BUs most similar to initial BU
- Facilitate peer-to-peer reference calls
- Offer pilot programs or proof of concepts
- Fast-track deployment with lessons learned

**Stage 3: Majority Adoption (Month 7-12)**
- Momentum from 2-3 BU successes
- Corporate leadership sees pattern of value
- Laggard BUs feeling peer pressure
- Competitive risk if {{competitors}} winning other BUs

**Stage 4: Enterprise Standard (Month 12-18)**
- Corporate mandate or strong guidance
- Holdout BUs converting
- Full enterprise-wide deployment
- Strategic partnership status

**Navigating Blockers:**

**Common Blocker Types:**

**The "Not Invented Here" Leader:**
- Resists anything not their idea
- Strategy: Make them feel like partner in decision, incorporate feedback, give credit

**The Budget Guardian:**
- Protects departmental budget fiercely
- Strategy: Show ROI specific to their BU, offer flexibility in deployment timing

**The Competitor Advocate:**
- Already using {{competitors}} solution
- Strategy: Coexistence approach (integrate vs. replace), pilot for neutral evaluation

**The Risk-Averse IT Leader:**
- Fears security, integration, support issues
- Strategy: Comprehensive security docs, integration proof points, phased rollout

## STEP 6: ENTERPRISE-WIDE DEPLOYMENT

Successfully managing large-scale rollout across complex organization.

### **DEPLOYMENT PLANNING**

**Rollout Approaches:**

**Approach A: Phased by BU (Sequential)**
- Deploy fully in BU 1 → BU 2 → BU 3
- Pros: Learn and refine, manageable scope per phase
- Cons: Slower overall timeline
- Best for: Complex integration, high-touch deployment

**Approach B: Concurrent Deployment (Parallel)**
- Deploy across all BUs simultaneously
- Pros: Faster time to full enterprise value, create momentum
- Cons: Resource-intensive, higher risk
- Best for: Standardized product, SaaS with low touch

**Approach C: Hub and Spoke (Lighthouse)**
- Deploy in 2-3 strategic BUs (hubs) first
- Other BUs (spokes) follow proven model
- Pros: Balance speed and learning, create champions
- Best for: Most enterprise deployments

**Change Management:**

Enterprise expansion = organizational change initiative:

**Executive Communication:**
- CEO email announcing strategic partnership
- Town halls explaining why and benefits
- Executive talking points for team meetings

**Department Leader Enablement:**
- Training on {{product_description}} value props
- FAQs and objection handling
- Success metrics and reporting

**End User Adoption:**

- Champions network (power users as peer advocates)
- Gamification and incentives for early adoption
- Help desk and support resources
