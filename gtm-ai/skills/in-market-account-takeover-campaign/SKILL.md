⚙️ **COMPREHENSIVE CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE**

**SECTION 1: SIGNAL DETECTION & ACCOUNT ACTIVATION**

**Intent Signal Monitoring Framework**
Your account takeover campaign depends on precise signal detection across digital touchpoints. {{company_name}} should monitor the following buying signal categories within your {{geographic_markets}} target accounts:

Research & Evaluation Signals:
- Website visits to product pages, pricing pages, and comparison content
- Downloads of technical resources, case studies, and ROI calculators
- Time spent on pages related to {{key_goals}} and core use cases
- Search behavior indicating competitive evaluation of {{competitors}}
- Whitepaper and solution brief downloads in your solution category

Engagement & Authority Signals:
- LinkedIn profile views from target account domains
- Engagement with your content (shares, comments, follows)
- Attendance at webinars or virtual events in your category
- Newsletter subscription or content downloads
- Participation in industry discussions related to your {{industry}}

Organizational Activity Signals:
- Job openings posted by target account in technical or operations roles
- Company announcements of funding, M&A, or expansion
- Budget cycle indicators (fiscal year-end planning, board meetings)
- Team growth patterns indicating platform scaling needs
- Executive changes in {{target_personas}} roles

Activation Threshold & Timing:
Signal density crossing is triggered when any target account generates 3+ buying signals within a consecutive 7-day window. Upon threshold crossing, execute the following immediately:

1. Real-time Alert Generation: Marketing automation platform flags account for Sales team
2. Account Context Assembly: Pull relevant account intelligence including industry vertical, current tech stack, competitive positioning within {{industry}}
3. Signal Mapping: Identify which specific signals triggered activation and create messaging hooks from that data
4. Recommended Messaging Angles: Identify 2-3 value drivers that connect to detected research topics
5. Sales Alert Distribution: Notify assigned AE, SDR, and account team with prioritized action items

**Signal-to-Messaging Translation Framework**
Map detected signals to tailored outreach angles:

IF research signal = comparison content + competitor research → THEN emphasize differentiation vs {{competitors}}, highlight {{company_name}} strengths on key evaluation criteria
IF engagement signal = job postings for engineering roles → THEN position solution as enabler for scaling technical teams, reference {{average_deal_size}} impact on operational efficiency
IF organizational signal = budget cycle indicators → THEN lead with ROI impact tied to {{product_pricing_model}}, emphasize {{customer_acquisition_cost}} efficiency gains
IF research signal = technical documentation downloads → THEN provide advanced implementation guidance, demonstrate integration capability with existing tech stacks

**SECTION 2: MULTI-CHANNEL CAMPAIGN EXECUTION**

**Phase 1: Initial Contact Blitz (Days 1-3)**

Critical Success Factor: Coordinated multi-channel launch within 24 hours of signal detection. All channels must reference the same research activity to create cohesive, intelligent outreach that demonstrates {{company_name}} understands their specific situation.

LinkedIn Executive Outreach (Day 1 morning):
Objective: Establish personal connection with authority and demonstrate insider knowledge

Execution:
- Assigned Account Executive or SDR (matched to prospect seniority) sends personalized connection request
- Connection message template (max 300 characters, using {{brand_voice}}):
  "{{prospect_name}}, I noticed {{company_name}} has been evaluating {{solution_category}} solutions — specifically your research into {{detected_research_topic}}. We've helped similar {{industry}} companies in {{geographic_markets}} achieve {{key_goals}} by [specific approach]. Would love to share how we're approaching this differently."

Message Customization Checklist:
  ✓ Reference the specific research signal that triggered activation (e.g., "your recent evaluation of scalable data infrastructure")
  ✓ Position solution value aligned to detected pain point (from signal research patterns)
  ✓ Include geographic or industry-specific social proof (customers in {{geographic_markets}} or {{industry}})
  ✓ Tone aligned with {{brand_voice}} (professional, consultative, not salesy)
  ✓ Avoid URLs in connection message — focus on credibility building

LinkedIn Connection Guidelines:
  - Send connection from assigned AE or senior SDR (not generic sales account)
  - Timing: 9am-11am in prospect timezone on business days
  - Personalization rate target: 100% of outreach (zero templated messages)
  - Follow-up engagement: Like/comment on 2-3 prospect posts within 2 days to build relationship momentum

Email Value-Focused Outreach (Day 1, 2-3 hours after LinkedIn):
Objective: Deliver compelling business case with specific relevance to detected buying signals

Execution:
- Subject line strategy (leverage {{brand_voice}} guidelines):
  - Formula: [Specific signal] + [Unexpected insight] = compelling open
  - Example: "{{prospect_name}} — Your {{detected_research_topic}} research + 3 things we're seeing"
  - Avoid: Generic subject lines, superlatives ("Amazing," "Incredible"), urgency/scarcity tactics
  
- Email body structure (150-200 words):
  1. Signal-specific opening (1-2 sentences): Reference detected activity without being creepy
     "I noticed {{company_name}} has been researching {{detected_research_topic}} — a topic we're helping {{industry}} leaders navigate in {{geographic_markets}}."
  
  2. Pain point validation (2-3 sentences): Connect research signal to business impact
     "Based on companies we work with, this evaluation usually surfaces {{implied_pain_point}} as a critical blocker. {{company_name}}'s expansion into {{geographic_markets}} makes this particularly acute."
  
  3. Unexpected insight (2-3 sentences): Provide genuinely useful perspective not obvious from marketing
     "What we're seeing that might surprise you: {{counterintuitive_insight}} — it's changing how {{industry}} players approach {{key_goals}}."
  
  4. Social proof attachment (1-2 sentences): Provide highly relevant case study
     "I've attached a case study from {{competitor}} in {{geographic_markets}} that achieved {{quantified_outcome}} — they were exactly where you are 9 months ago."
  
  5. Clear CTA (1 sentence): Low-friction meeting proposal
     "Worth a 15-minute conversation? Let's discuss how {{company_name}} can {{specific_value_promise}}."
  
  6. Signature: Include title, company, phone number (enable callback), LinkedIn URL

Email Attachment: One case study maximum, highly specific to detected use case
  - Case study positioning: Customer in {{industry}}, {{geographic_markets}}, similar {{average_deal_size}} range
  - Metrics highlighted: {{key_goals}} improvement, {{customer_acquisition_cost}} impact, implementation timeline
  - Format: 1-2 page PDF with clear problem-solution-results structure

Account-Based Display Execution:
  - Platform: LinkedIn ads, programmatic display (Google, Microsoft, etc.), or intent platforms

    * Daily budget: $50-150 depending on account size and {{average_deal_size}}

  ✓ LinkedIn message sent and documented in CRM with timestamp
  ✓ Email opened metric monitored (if available) within 4 hours

**Phase 2: Reinforcement & Authority Building (Days 4-7)**

Goal: Shift from initial awareness to deeper engagement, establish {{company_name}} as knowledgeable partner

Phone Outreach (Days 4-5):
Objective: Move conversation from written word to real-time dialogue, calibrate interest level and decision timeline

SDR Talk Track Framework (using {{brand_voice}}):
Opening (15 seconds):
  "Hi {{prospect_name}}, this is {{sdr_name}} from {{company_name}}. I know you're likely evaluating a few options right now for {{detected_research_topic}} — I wanted to reach out because I saw your team specifically looking at {{specific_research_aspect}}, which is exactly what's changed for {{industry}} leaders we work with. Do you have 15 minutes?"

If Yes - Proceed to Qualification:
  "Great. Quick context: I'm not here to pitch — I'm here to understand your situation better. When you look at {{key_goals}}, what's the biggest blocker you're hitting? [Listen for 30 seconds]. What we're seeing with similar-stage companies like {{company_name}}'s customers is that {{insight_from_research}}. How are you thinking about that?"

If No / Not Now - Establish Callback:
  "Completely understand. I'll send you something specific to what you're evaluating — just a one-pager on how companies in {{industry}} approach {{detected_pain_point}}. How about we grab 20 minutes next [specific day]?"

Call Handling Contingencies:
  - Voicemail template (use {{brand_voice}}, 25 seconds max):
    "{{prospect_name}}, this is {{sdr_name}} from {{company_name}}. I noticed your team is evaluating {{detected_research_topic}} and came across your recent research into {{specific_aspect}}. I've worked with {{industry}} leaders in {{geographic_markets}} solve exactly this. Let's connect briefly — {{phone_number}}."
  
  - Gatekeeper Response (to EA/assistant):
    "Hi, I'm {{sdr_name}} from {{company_name}}. I'm following up on something {{prospect_name}} was researching — {{detected_research_topic}} — just trying to get 15 minutes on their calendar this week. Is next Tuesday or Wednesday better?"

Call Cadence:
  - Attempt 1 (Day 4, 10am): Initial outreach
  - Attempt 2 (Day 5, 2pm): Follow-up attempt
  - If no connection: Send SMS (if available) or LinkedIn message with Calendly link as fallback

Objective: Establish {{company_name}} thought leadership, stay top-of-mind during evaluation

Content Strategy (3 posts over 3 days):
  1. Day 5: Share relevant industry research/trend aligned to {{detected_research_topic}}
     - Format: Article + custom commentary (using {{brand_voice}})
     - Tag prospect if appropriate (not always necessary)
     - Engagement goal: Trigger prospect engagement/comment (proof of continued attention)
  
  2. Day 6: Customer win story relevant to {{industry}} and {{key_goals}}
     - Format: Short case study post + link to full study
     - Storytelling angle: Outcome focused, specific metrics, timeline transparency

  3. Day 7: {{company_name}} differentiator content addressing {{competitors}} or industry misconception
     - Format: Carousel post, article, or video (if available)
     - Value angle: "The {{detected_research_topic}} evaluation mistake most {{industry}} teams make (and how to fix it)"
     - Credibility builder: Data-backed, specific to {{geographic_markets}} or industry vertical

LinkedIn Engagement Protocol:
  - Like/comment on prospect's posts if publicly shared (subtly reinforce presence)
  - Monitor if prospect engages with {{company_name}} content (positive signal of continued interest)
  - Avoid over-mentioning in comments (unnecessary, can feel forced)

Email Follow-Up (Day 6):
Objective: Re-engage if no email response, shift message angle based on initial performance

Follow-Up Email Strategy:
  - If email opened but no response (golden signal of interest):
    Subject: "Quick addition to the {{detected_research_topic}} conversation"
    Body: Provide additional resource, offer specific next step, reduce friction
    Attachment: Technical brief or ROI calculator aligned to detected pain point
  
  - If email not opened:
    Subject: "{{prospect_name}} — {{company_name}} approach to {{detected_pain_point}}"
    Body: Different angle, higher specificity to {{industry}} or {{geographic_markets}}, emphasize {{key_goals}} impact
    Attachment: Different asset (research report vs. case study, for example)
  
  - If email opened + prospect engaged with LinkedIn content:
    Subject: "Saw you reading about {{specific_content_topic}} — relevant to your work?"
    Body: Build on content engagement, position AE/SDR as knowledgeable resource, propose brief consultation
    Attachment: Deeper technical resource, whitepaper, or competitive analysis

**Phase 3: Conversion Push & Decision Facilitation (Days 8-14)**

Goal: Move qualified accounts to committed meeting, establish executive sponsorship, clarify buying timeline

Account Executive Engagement (Days 8-10):
Objective: Shift from SDR prospecting to executive-level consultation

Warm Handoff Protocol:
  - SDR prepares brief for AE: Account context, signal history, prospect engagement level, discovered pain points, recommended value angle
  - AE reviews prospect LinkedIn profile, recent activity, company news within {{geographic_markets}}
  - AE sends personalized LinkedIn message or email (not phone first) from position of peer/expert, not salesperson

AE Message Template (using {{brand_voice}}):
  "{{prospect_name}}, {{sdr_name}} mentioned you're in the middle of evaluating {{solution_category}} for {{key_goals}} — a challenge I work on constantly with {{industry}} leaders in {{geographic_markets}}. Rather than pitch, I'd like to understand your evaluation criteria and timeline. Specifically, {{custom_question_based_on_signals}}.

  I'm attaching a brief on how we help {{industry}} teams like {{company_name}}'s customers avoid the common mistakes. Would you be open to a brief call next week where I can understand what success looks like for you, rather than telling you about {{company_name}}?"

Executive-Level Value Messaging:
  - Focus on {{key_goals}} business impact, not feature discussion
  - Reference {{average_deal_size}} or revenue implications of decision
  - Quantify {{customer_acquisition_cost}} improvement or implementation ROI
  - Acknowledge competitive alternatives without disparaging (sophistication signal)
  - Position decision timeline and resource requirements

Phone Call: Executive Business Conversation (Days 9-10):
Objective: Qualify opportunity, understand budget authority, establish executive sponsor relationships

Call Structure (30 minutes ideal):

Opening (3 minutes): Establish credibility & discovery intent
  - "Thanks for making time, {{prospect_name}}. I know {{sdr_name}} reached out about your {{detected_research_topic}} evaluation. Rather than pitch {{company_name}}, I want to understand your situation — specifically where you are in the evaluation, what {{key_goals}} mean for your {{organization}}, and how we can be helpful. Sound good?"

Discovery (12 minutes): Understand buying situation deeply
  Key questions to ask (tailored to {{industry}}, {{geographic_markets}}, {{stage}}):
  1. "Walk me through what triggered the evaluation for {{detected_research_topic}} right now."
  2. "What specific {{key_goals}} outcome would make this investment successful for your team?"
  3. "What other solutions are you looking at, and why did {{company_name}} make the short list?" [Reveals evaluation criteria]
  4. "From your perspective, what's the ideal timeline to make a decision?" [Budget vs. preference]
  5. "Beyond you, who else is involved in the buying decision?" [Identify buying committee]
  6. "What could we show you this week that would move the conversation forward?" [Next steps clarity]

Consultation (10 minutes): Position value, establish AE as trusted advisor
  - Based on answers above, provide 2-3 specific insights relevant to their situation (not generic)
  - Ask permission: "Can I share how we've thought about this for {{industry}} peers in {{geographic_markets}}?"
  - Reference specific customer win / case study demonstrating {{company_name}}'s approach
  - Discuss implementation approach, timeline, resource requirements, ROI measurement

Close (5 minutes): Advance to next stage with clear action items
  - If qualified: "Based on this conversation, it sounds like {{company_name}} might be a fit. What I'd suggest is a quick product walkthrough with {{product_expert}} focused on {{specific_capability}}. How does Tuesday or Wednesday work?"
  - If not yet qualified: "I want to make sure this is worth your time. What if I send you a brief technical comparison on {{specific_dimension}} we discussed? Let's reconnect end of week and see if moving to a demo makes sense."

Call Documentation (CRM critical):
  ✓ Opportunity stage (Discovery, Solution fit, Evaluation, Proposal, etc.)
  ✓ Identified pain points vs. {{key_goals}}
  ✓ Buying committee members by name, role, influence level
  ✓ Budget range and timing (if discussed)
  ✓ Key objections or concerns
  ✓ Next steps with specific date/action owner
  ✓ Competitor intelligence (other solutions evaluated)

Email: Direct Meeting Proposal (Days 9-11):
Objective: If phone connection made, send calendar link immediately. If no phone connection, use email to drive meeting commitment.

Email Structure:
Subject: "{{prospect_name}} — Next steps on {{key_goals}} evaluation"
Body:
  "Following up on {{sdr_name}}'s outreach about your {{detected_research_topic}} evaluation. Based on your focus on {{key_goals}}, I think a conversation with our {{product/solution}} expert could be valuable.

  Rather than generic product demo, we'd focus on:
  - {{industry}}-specific {{key_goals}} challenges (relevant to {{geographic_markets}})
  - How {{company_name}} approach differs from {{competitors}}
  - Quick implementation estimate and ROI for {{company_name}}'s situation

  I've attached a pre-call brief on {{specific_topic}}. How does [specific day/time] for 30 minutes work?

  [Calendly link or specific calendar invite]"

Calendar Tactics:
  - Offer 2-3 specific time slots (reduces friction)
  - Propose 30 min max (signals respect for time, less intimidating)
  - Include agenda in calendar invite (clarity + commitment signal)
  - Enable video conferencing (Zoom/Teams), confirm tech in advance

LinkedIn Video Message (Days 12-13):
Objective: Last-mile engagement if meeting hasn't been booked, personalized urgency without pressure

Video Message Strategy:
  - Length: 30-45 seconds maximum
  - Tone: Warm, conversational, using {{brand_voice}}
  - Content: Personal context-setting + specific value proposition + easy next step
  
  Script Template:
  "{{prospect_name}}, this is {{ae_name}} from {{company_name}}. I know you're heads-down evaluating {{detected_research_topic}} right now. I just wanted to drop a quick note because I found a {{case_study/research/benchmark}} specific to {{industry}} and {{geographic_markets}} that directly connects to what you shared about {{discovered_pain_point}}. I think it's worth a quick conversation. Let me know what works."

  Delivery: Send via LinkedIn message (not email), increases engagement vs. text-based message

**SECTION 3: CHANNEL-SPECIFIC GUIDELINES & PERSONALIZATION PROTOCOLS**

**Email Channel Deep-Dive**

Email is your persistent, documented communication layer. Every {{company_name}} touchpoint should reinforce signal-based understanding.

Template Architecture (maintain {{brand_voice}} throughout):
  - Header: Company logo, sender name/title, company
  - Opening: Signal reference + personalization hook
  - Body: 3-5 sentences maximum, benefits over features
  - Social proof: 1 highly relevant case study or customer reference
  - CTA: Single, clear call-to-action (calendar link > generic "reply")
  - Signature: Full contact info + LinkedIn URL
  - Footer: Unsubscribe (required for compliance)

Personalization Minimum Standards:
  ✓ Use {{prospect_name}} minimum 2x in email body
  ✓ Reference {{company_name}} (target account name) by name, not "your company"
  ✓ Include specific {{detected_research_topic}} reference (the signal that triggered outreach)
  ✓ Align value prop to {{key_goals}} or {{detected_pain_point}}
  ✓ Include {{industry}} or {{geographic_markets}} reference for context relevance
  ✗ Do NOT use generic template language ("We help companies...")
  ✗ Do NOT include URL shorteners (trust killer)
  ✗ Do NOT use multiple CTAs (confuses recipient)

Email Send Optimization:
  - Send window: Tuesday-Thursday, 9am-11am or 2pm-3pm prospect timezone
  - Avoid: Monday (email overload), Friday afternoon (lost in weekend prep)
  - Frequency: Max 1 email every 2-3 days (avoid bombardment)
  - Sequence: LinkedIn first (relationship building) → Email follow-up (documentation) → Phone (commitment)
  - Tracking: Monitor opens, clicks, bounce rates; adjust messaging if metrics decline

**LinkedIn Channel Deep-Dive**

LinkedIn is your relationship layer. Use this channel to demonstrate expertise, build rapport, and stay visible during evaluation window.

Connection Strategy:
  - Connection timing: Immediate, within 1 hour of signal detection
  - Use personal name (not sales generic account)
  - Message in connection request (25-300 characters)
  - Engagement cadence: Like/comment on prospect posts 2-3x per week (subtly reinforce presence)
  - Content sharing: Share 2-3 {{company_name}} posts per week (authority building)

LinkedIn Outreach Messaging:
  - Avoid: "Would love to connect!" (worthless)
  - Use: "Noticed your research into {{detected_research_topic}} — we're helping {{industry}} leaders in {{geographic_markets}} approach this differently."
  - Tone: Peer, expert, helpful (not "We can help you!")
  - Pattern: Reference specific signal + value angle + soft ask

Video Message Best Practices (LinkedIn video feature):
  - Length: 30-45 seconds
  - Setup: Professional background, good lighting, steady camera
  - Content: Authentic, conversational, signal-specific
  - Call to action: "Let's grab 15 minutes," not "Buy now"
  - Engagement lift: 3-5x higher engagement vs. text-based message

Thought Leadership Positioning:
  - Share 2-3 posts per week during {{in_market_accounts}} lifecycle
  - Content themes: Industry trends, {{key_goals}} insights, {{company_name}} customer wins
  - Engagement strategy: Comment on prospect posts, industry posts, {{competitor}} discussions
  - Credibility signal: Original insight + data reference + relevance to {{geographic_markets}}/{{industry}}

Creative Development Framework:
  1. Pain-Point Response Creative: Visual + headline addressing {{detected_pain_point}}
     - Example: "For {{industry}} teams scaling {{key_goals}}" + benefit image
     - Body copy: 1-2 sentence benefit statement, CTA to resource
  
  2. Solution Capability Creative: Product screenshot + differentiator positioning
     - Headline: "Here's how {{company_name}} does {{solution_category}} differently"
     - Body: 1-2 feature/benefit callouts specific to {{key_goals}} impact
  
  3. Customer Success Creative: Customer logo/quote + quantified outcome
     - Headline: "{{company_name}} customer achieved {{specific_outcome}}"
     - Body: Implementation timeline + ROI reference aligned to {{average_deal_size}}
  
  4. Thought Leadership Creative: Research finding + industry credibility
     - Headline: "What {{industry}} leaders are doing differently in {{geographic_markets}}"
     - Body: Key insight from research + relevance to target buyer

Creative Specifications (all platforms):
  - Headline: 3-6 words, benefit-focused, no superlatives
  - Body text: 1-2 sentences max, conversational using {{brand_voice}}
  - CTA button: "Learn more," "Download brief," "See comparison" (action-oriented)
  - Image: 1200x628px minimum, authentic (no generic stock photos), {{company_name}} brand-aligned
  - Landing page: Custom takeover LP with no navigation, single offer, {{prospect_company}} or {{industry}} mention

Pacing & Duration:
  - Start: Day 1 of campaign (immediately with LinkedIn + email blitz)
  - Duration: 7 days (days 1-7), pause after email follow-up
  - Frequency cap: 2-3x impressions per person per day (avoid ad fatigue)
  - Daily budget: Allocate 10-15% of total campaign CAC budget ($50-150/day typical)

Performance Monitoring:
  - Key metrics: Impression share, CTR, cost per visit to landing page, audience overlap with email opens
  - Optimization: Pause underperforming creatives by Day 4, double-down on 1.5%+ CTR
  - Attribution: Track landing page visitors → CRM to correlate with email engagement + meeting booking

**Phone Channel Deep-Dive**

Phone is your highest-touch channel, enabling real-time qualification and relationship building.

SDR Qualification Criteria (before first call):
  ✓ Account fits {{tier_1_accounts}} or {{tier_2_accounts}} profile
  ✓ 3+ buying signals detected in past 7 days
  ✓ {{target_personas}} identified as decision-maker or influencer
  ✓ No recent contact history (within past 30 days) to avoid duplicate outreach
  ✓ Contact information verified (email match to domain, LinkedIn profile confirmation)

Call Timing Strategy:
  - Attempt 1: Day 4, 10am-11am (prospect timezone) — Primary SDR call
  - Attempt 2: Day 5, 2pm-3pm (prospect timezone) — Secondary SDR call
  - Attempt 3: Day 8-9, 10am (AE call if SDR qualified opportunity) — Executive conversation
  - Voicemail left on first attempt, no voicemail on second attempt (changes approach)
  - SMS fallback: If 2 call attempts unsuccessful, send LinkedIn message or SMS with Calendly link

SDR Call Objectives:
  - Confirm buying signal legitimacy (is this real evaluation or false positive?)
  - Identify decision maker + buying committee composition
  - Understand {{key_goals}} priority + timeline
  - Uncover {{detected_pain_point}} severity + business impact
  - Position {{company_name}} differentiator vs. {{competitors}} (if mentioned)
  - Advance to demo/consultation with AE (if qualified)

Talk Track Structure (adapt to {{company_name}}, {{industry}}, {{geographic_markets}}, {{brand_voice}}):
  
  Voicemail (25 seconds, friendly + professional):
  "{{prospect_name}}, this is {{sdr_name}} from {{company_name}}. I noticed your team is evaluating {{detected_research_topic}} — something we help {{industry}} companies in {{geographic_markets}} solve every day. Would love to chat briefly about your situation and see if there's a fit. {{sdr_phone}}. Looking forward to connecting."
  
  Call Opening (if prospect answers, 15 seconds):
  "Hi {{prospect_name}}, this is {{sdr_name}} from {{company_name}}. I know this might be out of the blue — I'm calling because I saw {{company_name}} is evaluating {{detected_research_topic}}, which aligns perfectly with what we do. Do you have about 15 minutes? If not now, when works better this week?"
  
  If Prospect Says "No" or "Busy":
  "No problem at all. I'll send you a quick one-pager on {{detected_research_topic}} that's specific to {{industry}} in {{geographic_markets}}. How about I follow up with you early next week — does Tuesday or Wednesday morning work better?"
  
  If Prospect Says "Yes" - Qualification (12-15 minutes):
  1. Situation: "So, walk me through {{company_name}}'s situation right now with {{detected_research_topic}}. What's triggering the evaluation?"
  2. Implications: "[Listen for 30 seconds, then] What would solving this mean for your {{key_goals}} and {{company_name}}'s bottom line?"
  3. Alternative approaches: "How are you thinking about solving this? Any other solutions you're looking at?"
  4. Timeline: "What's your timeline to make a decision and get something in place?"
  5. Buying committee: "Beyond you, who else is involved in this decision?"
  6. Desired outcome: "What could we show you that would move this conversation forward?" [Gets specific next step]
  
  If Qualified - Transition to AE:
