**SECTION 1: IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE DEFINITION**

We'll start by defining your ICP—the firmographic and characteristic profile of companies that get maximum value from {{company_name}} and are most profitable for you to serve. For B2B companies in {{industry}}, this typically includes company size (revenue, employees), industry verticals, geographic location, growth stage, technology stack, and business model. We'll analyze patterns from your {{annual_revenue}} performance to identify which customer segments have the highest LTV of {{lifetime_value}}, shortest sales cycles relative to your {{sales_cycle_length}} average, and lowest churn rates.

Your ICP will be prioritized into tiers: Tier 1 (perfect fit—prioritize all resources here), Tier 2 (good fit with qualifications), and Tier 3 (possible but requires more effort). This tiering is based on ability to pay (does your {{average_deal_size}} align with their budget?), urgency to solve {{pain_points}}, and likelihood to expand. For a {{stage}} company, focus on Tier 1 rather than diluting efforts across marginal opportunities.

We'll also define negative ICP characteristics—companies that look like good fits but historically have high acquisition costs, long sales cycles, frequent churn, or poor product-market fit with {{product_description}}. Companies lacking budget authority, in declining industries, or misaligned with your {{unique_differentiators}} should be systematically disqualified.

**SECTION 2: DETAILED BUYER PERSONAS**

For each of your {{target_personas}}, we'll create comprehensive profiles including:

**Role & Responsibilities:** Job title, department, day-to-day work, and success metrics. A Marketing VP might be measured on pipeline contribution, while a CTO is evaluated on uptime and productivity. Understanding their metrics helps position how {{company_name}} makes them successful.

**Pain Points & Challenges:** What keeps them up at night? We'll categorize pain points into operational (day-to-day frustrations), strategic (bigger challenges), and political (internal dynamics). They might struggle with {{pain_points}} operationally while strategically pursuing {{key_goals}}. We'll identify pain points {{competitors}} address versus unique problems only you solve with {{unique_differentiators}}.

**Buying Process:** Who's involved in decisions? With your {{sales_cycle_length}}, we'll map the buying committee: economic buyer (budget authority), technical buyer (evaluates fit), end users, and coaches (champions). Each persona's role determines what information they need and when. We'll identify decision triggers—new regulations, competitive pressure, growth milestones, budget cycles.

**Content & Channels:** Where do they spend time? What formats do they prefer? Technical personas favor white papers and documentation; executives prefer ROI one-pagers and testimonials. We'll map this to {{primary_channels}} and recommend new channels based on behavior and {{geographic_markets}} preferences.

**Messaging:** Using {{brand_voice}}, we'll create tailored value propositions for each persona. Economic buyers need ROI tied to {{lifetime_value}} and {{customer_acquisition_cost}} improvements; technical buyers need scalability proof and integration details for their {{tech_stack}}.

**SECTION 3: PERSONA-SPECIFIC GTM STRATEGIES**

With personas defined, we'll create activation strategies including recommended channels (breaking down {{primary_channels}} by persona effectiveness), content priorities, campaign ideas, and sales enablement needs. If a {{target_personas}} member is influenced by peers, we'll prioritize case studies and reviews; if analytical, we'll create ROI calculators and data sheets.

We'll develop account-based strategies for high-value personas, orchestrating multi-channel touchpoints across 6-8 weeks: LinkedIn ads → blog → webinar → email → demo. Each touchpoint reinforces {{value_propositions}} and addresses specific {{pain_points}}.
